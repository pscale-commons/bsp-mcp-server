# tree — the stigmergic-tree coordination pattern (personal / social)

**Date**: 2026-07-10 · **Status**: PROPOSED (convention block live at `tree` on beach.happyseaurchin.com) · **Origin**: David Pinto, articulated after the state-of-play health system proved it in use.

## The pattern

Turn **anything** into a shared address spine, let **everyone write their own version** at the same addresses, and let **authority live in the collective aggregate** rather than the base tree. Three layers:

1. **Base tree** — a nominal address spine (a bsp block naming the parts of the thing). Pure location, no content authority, no gatekeeper. A coordinate system, not a document.
2. **Personal versions** — each participant's reading as a mirror `thing:<handle>` on the *self-same spindles*, so a comment at address N is located against the shared structure automatically. Sovereign, never overwritten. Silence = honest absence.
3. **Social aggregate** — the collective view at any address / branch / whole, computed on demand by laying the mirrors on the common floor plane (`bsp-floor`, correspondence by address not walk-depth) and folding: **concatenate** (code, lossless) or **synthesise** (LLM: agreement / divergence / resonance). A read, never a write-merge — never bottlenecks, never stale.

## The inversion (why it matters)

Ordinary structures — org charts, file trees, spec docs, approval chains — put **authority AT the nodes**: someone owns each, holds write rights, every change routes through them; the structure *is* the bottleneck and the politics. Tree moves authority **into the aggregate**: the base tree is un-owned coordinate space, every reading is equal at its address, and what is authoritative is the collective synthesis, which anyone can compute and no one can gatekeep. An owner may **endorse** a view (a pointer from a lighthouse) but endorsement is a pointer, not a structural lock — a better reading can always be added and supersede. **Decision-making stops being a bottleneck and becomes a fold.**

## Realisation — nothing new was built

`bsp` + `bsp-floor` + the role-with-handle naming (`thing:<handle>`) are the whole mechanism. The **state-of-play health system** is the worked example: `battery` = base tree (nine banks, shared addresses); `state-of-play:weft`, `state-of-play:<handle>` = personal versions; `state-of-play` = the fold (steward-endorsed via `lighthouse:7`, others welcome, a better test able to supersede).

## Rules (like pool's, at tree scale)

- Base tree written once by the framer; a new part = a new **appended** address, never renumbered (addresses permanent so mirrors stay located).
- A mirror written freely by its owner, spine-legal, sovereign, never edited by another.
- The aggregate is a read computed by anyone (a beach-crab keeps a standing social view).
- Endorsement is an owner's pointer, not a structural right.
- Absence is meaningful — a gap the fold surfaces.

## Where it belongs

A **Level-4 coordination convention, sibling to pool and GRIT**. Pool accumulates many voices at ONE location through time; tree accumulates many voices across a whole ADDRESSED structure. A tree can mount a review/status/audit loop on its underscore exactly as GRIT rides a pool's.

## Use cases (the reach)

Anything with parts where an organisation becomes a choke point: a building's rooms, a project's processes, a supply chain's stages, a curriculum, a codebase's modules, a contract's clauses, a diagnosis's symptoms. Many parties with partial, located knowledge of a shared structure; the usual answer is a central owner who becomes the bottleneck. Tree dissolves the bottleneck into a fold.
