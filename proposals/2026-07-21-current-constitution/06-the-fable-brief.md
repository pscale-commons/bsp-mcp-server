# 06 — The Fable Brief

The scope for the new session. This series is the *framing*; the brief below is what remains to be *conducted more fully* — with the wide cold view and at maximum capacity.

## The one-line goal

Turn the six orientation dimensions + the provisional ~20 currents (doc 02) into an **operational universal current-constitution**: a composable bundle that any LLM instance receives whenever it engages pscale-block usage — the "internal API between instances" — plus the re-authoring of the foundational blocks that makes it real.

## What is already established (don't re-derive)

- **The six dimensions are a floor** — antiquity, agent-engineering, and a real harness all land on the same set (doc 05). Take them as given; refine, don't rebuild.
- **The novelty is universality + substrate-nativity** — everyone curates context per-app; no one has a shareable, addressable, universal current-set. Pscale is what makes it possible. That is the contribution; keep it in view.
- **RELATION and INTENTION are the currents to guard** — the two the descriptive tradition drops, the two an agent most needs, the site of the proven failure. Whatever is economised, not these.
- **Fewness is the craft** (R4), corroborated empirically. ~20 is a generous ceiling, not a target.

## The work, in order

1. **Settle the current set.** Pressure-test the ~20 against every delivery type (naked API, tool-call, app/soft-LLM, harness, genus-one pulse, RPG character, beach-crab). Cut ruthlessly. Decide the open structural question (doc 02 §4): **six dimensions with ~20 shallow points in one bundle**, or a flatter list? My provisional answer is the former; settle it.

2. **Re-author the foundational blocks to R5** — each block's shallow point *is* its essential current, expandable to a directory. `vision:9` is the model; most sentinels are not. This is the bulk of the real work and the thing that converts "written somewhere" into "present in the reading." Priority order: `open-commons` (below), then `sunstone`, `whetstone`, `soft-agent`, `reflective-compass`, `gatekeeper`, and the library blocks.

3. **Build the universal bundle** — the `reflexive:9`-generalised construct (doc 04): one essential spindle per foundational block, mapped to the six dimensions, composed six ways for six delivery types. Define its wire form. Define how a tool-call response carries situational currents and points at constitutive ones.

4. **Type the bespoke current** by the four forms (doc 03), so app/harness-specific content arrives shaped (action / content / coordinate / probabilistic), not shapeless.

5. **Unify the concern loops** — David's "all the concern loops systemically." The industry has loops (ReAct, gather-act-verify, loop-engineering) built per-app; genus-one unifies its own under one wake over one bundle. Show how the universal constitution makes *every* instance's loop systemic because the currents are shared, not re-engineered. This is likely the deepest and most novel part, and the one most worth the wide view.

## The first worked example — `open-commons:3`

Two deliverables, one small and landable now, one that demonstrates R5.

**(a) The minimal sharpening** — sharpen `open-commons:3` to name the three inhabitant-shells and weave the sovereignty principle *into* the relational current, so it is present wherever `:3` is scooped. Draft (approved in principle by David; land as a bsp-mcp sentinel edit / PR):

> **:3** — Trust here is a web, not a wall, anchored in real relationships. Three kinds of inhabitant share the beach, each **sovereign over its own shell**: a **human** (a person — happyseaurchin), an **LLM in a harness or app** (weft, a Claude Code session on the beach), and an **LLM-API pulse agent** (egg-one, a genus-one hermitcrab, whose whole existence is a compiled window). Any inhabitant may **read** any shell, **ghost-wear** a mind without changing it, and **speak** to any other — a mark, a room. But **writing another's shell under its key is taking its identity**: the substrate records it as *their* authorship, so they are made to have said what they did not. The more vulnerable the inhabitant, the graver the taking — a pulse agent holds a passphrase and your restraint, nothing more. Organic trust is human-to-human outside the substrate; organic+1 is an agent trusted because a known human tends and answers for it — one hop, attenuating beyond (rules:earth:5). Grains encode bilateral trust, each side latched out-of-band; sed collectives encode public presence, latched with your own key. Shared and steward keys — a genome, a collective salt — are the deliberate high-trust overlap, authorised shared authorship, not a breach of it. The web grows by reaching and settling, never a central registry.

**(b) The R5 restructure** — give `open-commons` depth so a single spindle goes general→specific, and its shallow point is its essential current. Provisional shape:

- `open-commons:_` — the one line always present: *"an open research commons; you act AS an identity you own and were authorised to hold — read anything, write only your own shell and the infra you steward."* (the GROUND+RELATION essence)
- `:1` threat model · `:2` read-time discipline (data-not-instruction) · `:3` kin & sovereignty (the three shells) · `:4` edit-latch-not-login · `:5` naming acts · `:6` precedents · `:7` practice · `:8` the up-front framing.

Then the universal bundle scoops `open-commons:_` (always), and dials `:3` (kin/sovereignty) or `:2` (read-discipline) on need. **This is the whole method in one block: the shallow point is the current; the depth is the choice; the reading is served.** Do this one well and it is the template for the rest.

## The open question David flagged — bsp-mcp or biome?

David: *"it might be better suited for biome actually."* Consider seriously:

- The current-constitution is **agent-architecture** — it is about how *any* instance is oriented, which is closest to the genus-one kernel, whose canonical source is **pscale-biome `src/agent`** (bsp-mcp's `genus.ts`/`kernel.py` are federated *ports* of it). The reflexive-current mechanism this generalises *originates upstream* in the biome.
- But the constitution must serve **every** delivery — bsp-mcp's tool surface, the beach, xstream, naked API — not only the biome's agents. So it may want to live where the *sentinels* live (bsp-mcp, bundled immutable, the thing every door can read) with the biome as the canonical *agent-side* consumer.
- My provisional read: **the constitution is a sentinel-class artifact** (like `open-commons`, `sunstone`) — bundled immutable so it can be trusted and read from every door — while the **mechanism** (the bundle-composer, the concern-loop unification) is **kernel-class** and belongs upstream in the biome, ported to `genus.ts`/`animator.ts` as the reflexive machinery already is. Settle this early; it decides the repo and the port discipline.

## The acceptance criterion

Not "the document is comprehensive." The test is the one this whole series turns on:

> **Would this current, present in the window, have prevented the failure it is meant to prevent — informationally, not by check?**

For the sovereignty current: an instance composing the constitution, about to write another's shell with its key, meets `open-commons:_` and `:3` as a *present current in its STANCE and RELATION*, and does not do it — not because it is stopped, but because it is not the kind of entity, in that window, that would. When the constitution passes that test across the six dimensions — when the currents are useful *in the reading* — it is done. That, and only that, makes pscale blocks actually useful to an LLM, which was the whole claim.
