# 04 — Design Rules

The rules that make a current-constitution *work in the reading* rather than merely exist in the writing. Several are David's, stated across the project; two are corroborated by the whole agent-engineering field (doc 05); one is the mechanism that ties the series together.

---

## R1 — Reading over writing

The reading is the point; the writing serves it. A block is authored *for* the moment it is scooped into a window, not for the satisfaction of having been recorded. This inverts the ordinary instinct (write it down somewhere and the job is done) and is the direct lesson of this session's sovereignty failure: the rule was written in two places and present in none. **Every authoring decision is answerable to: does this improve the reading?** If a block cannot be well-read as a current, it is mis-authored, however correct its content.

## R2 — Depth, not flat fanning

Author *down a spindle*, general→specific, so a single walk arrives self-contextualised — never as a flat fan of sibling positions. A flat fan forces a whole-block dump to be understood; a deep spindle is read as a point, a walk, or a walk-plus-subdirectory, at whatever aperture the reader needs. Corollary (the standing "fan 5–7" rule): never fill 1–9 — a full fan has no legal sibling, and the next item lands in position 1's field and subnests it. **Leave headroom; author depth.** This is what makes a current *dialable* rather than all-or-nothing.

## R3 — The spindle is pre-decided; the aperture is the choice

Two coordinates, two different authorial acts.

- The **spindle** (S) is *settled context* — the path the author has already decided the reader should walk. It is composed into the window; the reader does not choose it.
- The **`pscale_attention`** (P) is the *aperture* — the dial between determinism and agency at the terminus. Shallow = one settled point (execution, Mode A); deeper = a sub-directory of companions the reader *chooses among* (deliberation, Mode B).

So a current is authored as: *this spindle, always; this depth, by default; deeper on the reader's need.* The settled part carries what must be true; the open part offers choice without imposing it. (This is the state-block reading — the block is the program, the walk is the electricity, `(S,P)` is the dial — applied to currents.)

## R4 — Fewness is the discipline, not the compromise

The always-present set must stay small. This is not economy for its own sake; it is the empirical constraint every agent-builder has hit independently (doc 05): the **"smallest possible set of high-signal tokens"** (Anthropic), because a window degrades when overfilled — "context rot," the "dumb zone" past ~40% fill. **The moment the constitutive set bloats, it stops being live currents and becomes a wadge of guardrails again — external by sheer volume.** Twenty is already generous. The test: if a current cannot be said in a line, it is not a current — it is a directory to leave dialable. Ruthless prioritisation is the whole craft; everything that is not *always necessary* is *dialable*, not *resident*.

## R5 — Each foundational block's shallow point *is* its essential current

This is the authoring mandate that makes R1–R4 buildable. Every sentinel and library block should be authored so that its **shallowest read — its `_`, or its `:1` — is the single line that must be present if the block is present at all**, with depth beneath for choice.

- `vision:9` already exemplifies it: it is authored *as* "the foundational ground — loaded every call as the constant backdrop." That is why it reads as the model. Author every foundational block to that standard.
- `open-commons` does *not* yet: it is eight flat paragraphs, excellent individually, with no single "if you read one line, read this" shallow point and no depth-dial. Re-authoring it to R5 is the concrete first task (doc 06).

The mandate in one sentence: **a block should be legible as a current at its shallow point, and expandable to a directory of choice, and only then to its whole self.** Point → walk → subtree, each a deliberate read, the shallow one always safe to compose.

## R6 — A current declares its form and its tempo

From doc 03: a current carries not just a payload but a *cognitive stance* (content / action / coordinate / probabilistic) and a *tempo* (constitutive / situational). The reader engages differently by form (know / do / locate / resolve) and by tempo (law / latest). Form-legibility is the read-time discipline generalised — knowing what *kind* of block you read is how you avoid reading data as instruction (the injection failure) or instruction as data (the inert-recipe failure). **Author the voice so the form and tempo are readable** (`sunstone:8`'s four signs plus the ±block sign already encode most of this).

## The mechanism — a universal bundle, `reflexive:9` generalised

The rules converge on one construct. Genus-one already has it: `reflexive:9` is *"the address bundle, stripped of semantics, that indexes the whole context window… every block at a dilation, from a single point to the whole block: settled context as depth, open choice as a directory… carried forward and re-dialed."* That is precisely the current-constitution — for one delivery type.

**The universal version is the same construct, delivery-agnostic:** a bundle that scoops **one essential spindle per foundational block, mapped onto the six dimensions**, always present, each dialable. A first sketch (to be settled by Fable against doc 02's ~20):

| dimension | essential spindles (shallow points) |
|---|---|
| **STANCE** | the inhabitant's identity/way block · `capabilities:_` · `whetstone:3` (face/tier) |
| **GROUND** | `sunstone:_` (the walk) · `open-commons:_` (the place) + `open-commons:3` (kin/sovereignty) · form-awareness |
| **INTENTION** | `vision:9` (the backdrop) · `purpose:_` |
| **SITUATION** | `located:_` · `sundial:8.2` (derive your now) · `conditions:_` |
| **RELATION** | `open-commons:3` (kin, sovereignty) · `relationships:1` (the holder) · who's-reaching |
| **GIVEN** | the gap · the ask |

How it composes per delivery type:

- **Naked API call** — constitutive currents baked into the *system*, situational into the *message*. (Exactly genus-one; exactly what a harness already does per doc 05.)
- **Tool-call response** — situational currents ride the *result envelope*; constitutive currents are assumed resident in the caller, or a pointer to them is returned (the shallow spindle, dialable).
- **App / soft-LLM** — the app composes the bundle for its mediating instance (the `soft-agent` shell is this, for one app; the universal makes it portable).
- **Harness (Claude Code)** — the CLAUDE.md + memory + system prompt already carry most currents; the bundle is what a *pscale-native* CLAUDE.md would be — currents by reference, dialable, shared.

The one construct, composed six ways for six delivery types, from one addressable set. That portability — *the same currents, wherever the instance runs* — is the "internal API between instances." It is what no per-app context-engineering can be, and what only an addressable substrate can provide.

---

## The rules as a checklist (for authoring or auditing any current)

1. Does it improve **the reading**, not just the record? (R1)
2. Is it authored **down a spindle**, with headroom, never a full flat fan? (R2)
3. Is the **spindle settled** and the **depth a dial**, not all-or-nothing? (R3)
4. Is it **necessary-always** — and if not, is it **dialable, not resident**? (R4)
5. Is its **shallow point the one essential line**, expandable to a directory? (R5)
6. Are its **form and tempo legible** in the voice? (R6)

A current that passes all six is *in-formational*: present in the reading, constitutive not regulative, and small enough to stay a current rather than swell into a guardrail.
